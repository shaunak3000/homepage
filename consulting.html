Consulting

From 2015 to 2021, as a Professor of Operations and Finance at TU Eindhoven, I applied academic-grade rigor to real business problems — consulting for Fortune 500 companies and SMEs across Europe, occasionally involving my research staff. Engagements ranged from focused analytical projects to long-term retainer relationships spanning multiple functions and markets, helping firms cut through complexity, redesign operations, and make better decisions under uncertainty.

Fortune 500 Chemicals Manufacturer
SimulationStatistical ForecastingOperations Strategy
Situation
Shipping chemicals from the Middle East to Europe; explore whether any efficiency gains are possible.
Task
1. Reduce shipping costs while incorporating demand forecasts.
2. Assess the general supply chain strategy.
Actions & Results
1. Smart ordering considering the sawtooth shipping prices, available inventory and projected demand.
2. Combining several shipping routes to southern Europe with those sent to northern Europe — achieving higher service levels as well as reduced costs.
Dutch Physiotherapy Clinics
SimulationStatistical ForecastingOperations Strategy
Situation
Rapid growth after acquiring rival clinics across the Netherlands; unable to keep up with capacity planning.
Task
Assess current capacity needs and efficiently allocate capacity while maximizing utilization.
Actions & Results
1. Improved data management practices (time stamping, therapist logs, classification) for future analyses.
2. Reduced treatment duration to 25 minutes for increased utilization.
3. Standardized therapist contracts for increased flexibility.
4. New treatment plan with initial screening from senior therapists; future appointments served by available therapists.

Dutch e-commerce Webshop
Combinatorial OptimizationForecasting
Situation
Growing demand led to setting up new warehouses, but product allocation needs to be revised.
Task
Allocate products to different warehouses while satisfying capacity constraints, assignment, warehouse exclusivity and firm-specific unique constraints.
Actions & Results
1. Switch from allocating suppliers to warehouse — to allocating products to warehouse.
2. Bundle frequently ordered products in the same warehouse.
3. New allocation is scalable with increases in warehouses.
4. Use a 60-day forecast for allocation, revised every two weeks.

Dutch Financial Institution — Personal Loans
Machine LearningA/B Testing
Situation
Personal loan applications go through a long vetting process. The bank would like to fast-track profitable applications.
Task
1. Determine the expected profitability of the loan based on application characteristics.
2. Examine the follow-up strategy on approved loans.
Actions & Results
1. Loan applications bucketed into 5 categories from high to low profitability.
2. Several loan application characteristics identified as "highly profitable".
3. A/B testing showed calling approved applicants after 7, 10, 15 days increased acceptance rate by 5%.

Synchromodal LSP
Stochastic OptimizationSimulation-based Optimization
Situation
The LSP can sell services in advance via allocation contracts or on the spot market. Allocation contracts lack volume constraints; the spot market is volatile but profitable.
Task
Determine the optimal mix between fixed and spot contracts while maximizing asset utilization.
Actions & Results
1. A profit maximization model works better than a maximize asset utilization model.
2. Substituting express shipment allocation to spot markets yields higher profitability.
3. Contracts to freight forwarders should incorporate volume constraints to reduce volatility.

Task
Examine why VMI shows disappointing performance in maintaining freshness in stores.

Online Fashion Returns Processing Firm
Performance AnalyticsDesignDistributed Decision Making
Situation
High return rates from online fashion sales require efficient processing. With rapid e-commerce growth in the EU, the firm wanted to set up a performance management system for its return sites.
Task
Determine the appropriate detail level and design the PMS for returns processing sites in EU.
Actions & Results
1. Three PMS levels implemented: individual site and organization each with unique KPIs at hour, shift, and month levels.
2. Efficiency gains possible if WMS of seller integrates with WMS of returns processing sites.
3. Invest in automation systems for counting, package processing and other manual tasks.
